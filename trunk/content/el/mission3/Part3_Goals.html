Part 3: Food Pyramid

Activities
3.1: Food pyramid information.
3.2: Food pyramid simulation.
3.3.1: The food diary of your character: Calculate daily caloric intake. The students will
compare their personal food diary (activity 1.4.1) with the daily required quantity   
(activity 2.1.1) and the food pyramid recommendations (activity 3.1) and derive  
conclusions on how healthy is their diet. In addition, the students will use this
information to complete their personal ‘health passport’ (activity 6.3).
3.3.2: Evaluate alter-ego/self diet in terms of the food pyramid.
________________________________________________

Description of Activity 3: In this activity, you will familiarize yourself with the food pyramid (old/new) and its function. In addition, you will learn how to construct the food pyramid by placing different food products in the appropriate pyramid group. Finally, you will compare your/alter-ego diet with the daily nutritional value and the food pyramid requirements.

General learning goals and skills
In this activity you will:

  • organize, analyze and interpret data.
  • collaborate with peers (share tasks/information, collaborate on experiments, make decision together, etc.).
  • write individual reports and assignments.
  • perform simple calculations (e.g. additions and multiplications).

Domain-specific learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • learn about the concept of the food pyramid (old and new) and its purpose.
  • learn about the different groups of food products and frequency of consumption.
  • learn how to construct a food pyramid by placing different food products in the correct group.
  • learn how to assess your own eating habits (health passport).
