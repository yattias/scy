Calculate the Estimated energy requirements (EER)

1. Compose a spreadsheet that looks like the example below.

  • In the first two columns you fill in all the activities you have performed in one particular day, as well as the duration of each activity (see the exercise diary). Please note that duration is given in hours; so 30 minutes equals 0.5 hours.
  • Check if the total duration of all the activities is 24 hours.
  • From the list of activities you copy the estimated amount of calories burned in one hour (while performing that particular activity). Enter these values in the third column. If you cannot find information in the list, check one of the websites (http://primusweb.com/fitnesspartner/calculat.htm and
    http://health.discovery.com/centers/cholesterol/activity/activity.html), or choose another activity from the list that you feel is approximately as energy consuming.
  • Now calculate the amount of calories burned for the duration of each activity (fourth column). Multiply the value in the second column by the value in the third column.
  • Calculate the total amount of energy spend during that day, by adding up all the values in the fourth column. This is the EER of that particular day.

2. Follow the same steps for each day in the exercise diary.

3. Calculate your average EER. This value will be used for your Health Passport.
