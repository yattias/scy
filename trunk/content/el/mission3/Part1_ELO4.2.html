1.4.2: Create a food and exercise diary
The goal of this mission is to create a healthy pizza. But in order to do that, you need to know some things. For instance, you have to be able to calculate the amount of nutrients and energy in a particular food item. You must also know how to estimate the amount of energy your body uses while performing a particular activity. You will practice all these skills during the mission. But if we want to do calculations, we need numbers. And you will create these data yourself.

  • Pick one character from the list of characters [link to list with descriptions]. This will be your alter-ego for the rest of the mission.
  • Based on the description of your character, you will create a detailed food and exercise diary. So, try to imagine what your character would eat and drink for breakfast, lunch and dinner. What kind of snacks would he or she consume (and how many)? And what kind of activities will he or she perform throughout the day?
  • Try to be as realistic as possible. You will need these data throughout the mission for further calculations.
  • Near the end of the mission (during activity 6) you will compile a health passport [link to health passport] for the character you have chosen.

Instructions for creating a food and exercise diary

  1. First check the example of a food and exercise diary to get an impression how it should look.
  2. Write down everything your character eats or drinks in a period of three days (from midnight to midnight).
  3. Not only write down the name of a particular food product, but also note the quantity he or she has consumed (in grams or litres).
  4. If you are not sure about what would be a realistic weight of a particular food item, you can:
  • check food packages at home or in a supermarket
  • use a (kitchen) scale or measuring cup to measure it
  • find the information you need on the internet

Once you start doing this, you will develop a certain feeling about how much things weigh (e.g. an average sized glass is about 200 ml).

  1. Note down everything your character does in a period of three days (from midnight to midnight).
  2. Not only write down what he or she is doing, but also how long he or she does it (in minutes).
  3. Even when your character is not really exercising, but just attending class or sleeping (or both at the same time), his or her body consumes energy. So, you also have to note these kinds of activities.
  4. If your character is performing the same activity twice during one hour (e.g. walking to the video store, and 20 minutes later walking back to your house) you can of course add these two values up.
