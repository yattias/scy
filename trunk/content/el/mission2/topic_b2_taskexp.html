Experimental procedure

Here you are provided with an experimental procedure developed by experts. You can use it in your experiment for getting valid results.

1. Weigh 4 g of Canadian Waterweed.
2. Set up the Halogen lamp.
3. Fill the beakers slowly with water (400 ml).
4. Place the beakers to different distances from the light source, the nearest beaker should be 50 cm from the lamp.
5. Set the measuring lenght (6 min) and the sampling rate (5 sec) of the Vernier data collector.
6. Measure the light intensity right next to the each beaker with the Light Sensor.
7. Make sure the concentration of dissolved oxygen in water is constant (below 2 mg/l) at the beginning of experiment.
8. Place the plants in to the beakers filled with water.
9. Place Dissolved Oxygen Probe in water and choose "Collect" from the data collector. Stir gently until the reading stabilizes. Take readings of oxygen concentration in each beaker.
