Description of Activity 7: In this activity you will create a second pizza and discus the steps you followed in doing so. Then you will be introduced to the optimization strategy using a simple application example. Following the example, you will have a chance to apply the optimization strategy using the pizza ingredients.

General learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • identify a problem and formulate hypotheses.
  • design, plan and perform experiments to test the hypotheses.
  • organize, analyze and interpret data.
  • collaborate with peers (share tasks/information, collaborate on experiments, make decision together, etc.).
  • reflect on your own knowledge and learning goals.
  • present in the presence of a large group (partners and/or classmates).
  • write individual reports and assignments.
  • perform simple calculations (e.g. additions and multiplications).

Domain-specific learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • created a healthy pizza based on the information attained from the previous activities.
  • present the methodology you followed in order to create a healthy pizza.
  • study an example of the optimization strategy.
  • investigate the information (nutrients, energy, cost) for the ingredients you selected in Activity 2, through a market research or through a web-search.
  • present the criteria you used in order to select the ingredients for your pizza.Â 
  • implement the optimization strategy in order to create a healthy pizza.
  • evaluate the optimization strategy.
  • answer questions concerning the importance of the criteria and explain your reasoning.
  • explain how the optimization strategy can be used to numerically weigh criteria.
