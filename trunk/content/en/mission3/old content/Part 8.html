Part 8: Create and Compare Pizzas

Activities
8.1: Creation of a healthy pizza.
8.2: Compare pizzas.
8.2.1: Compare pizzas among groups.
8.2.2: Optional: pizza taste competition.
8.3: Reflection.
________________________________________________

Description of Activity 8: Based on the optimization strategy, you will create a new healthy pizza and calculate its nutritional value. You will then compare all three pizzas you created throughout the mission. Finally, you will have the opportunity to compare and compete for the healthiest pizza among your peers.

General learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • identify a problem and formulate hypotheses.
  • organize, analyze and interpret data.
  • collaborate with peers (share tasks/information, collaborate on experiments, make decision together, etc.).
  • reflect on your own knowledge and learning goals.
  • present in the presence of a large group (partners and/or classmates).
  • write individual reports and assignments.

Domain-specific learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • create your final healthy pizza using the optimization strategy.
  • compare the pizzas you created throughout the mission and decide which is the healthiest.
  • compare your pizzas with those of other groups and compete for the healthiest and tastiest one.
  • respond to questions concerning the epistemological issues of the optimization strategy.

 

8.1: Creation of a healthy pizza.
Based on what you have studied so far and implementing the optimization strategy, create your third healthy pizza, using the same simulation tool. This time however, the simulation tool will carry out all the calculations for you, based on the weight value you assigned in the previous activity. Finally, you will have to calculate the nutrients and energy of your pizza and create a nutritional value label per serving (slice or /100g).


8.2: Compare pizzas you created.
Describe the strategy you followed to create each pizza. Which one of the three pizzas you created is healthier and why?

 

8.2.1: Compare pizzas among groups.
Compare your pizza with those of your peers and rank them based on healthiness and cost. Explain your reasoning and present your results to the rest of the class.


8.2.2: Pizza taste competition (optional)
If the participating schools have the suitable equipment and the appropriate building installations, students can attempt to bake a real pizza, using the ingredients they have selected. In addition, they can have a competition for the tastiest pizza in their class or school. If no equipment and/or facilities are available, the schools could try to contract a local restaurant to bake the students’ pizzas.

 

8.3: Reflection
Now that you are aware of the benefits of a healthy diet and the hidden dangers consuming food products high in fat, sugar and salt content, you should choose the healthier pizza you have created in order to present it to your school’s canteen/cafeteria. As a member of the student health committee at your school, write a letter to your school canteen/cafeteria (or school principle) presenting your pizza. Explain why your pizza provides a healthier option from what is sold/served right now at your school.
