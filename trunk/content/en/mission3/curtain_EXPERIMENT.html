Health Passport: You probably think you have a reasonable idea of what you eat and drink on an average day. But do you know how many grams of food you actually consume? How about the amount of fluids you drink? Finally, how much do you exercise? Well, now is the time to find out. For the duration of one day, you are going to keep track of everything you eat and drink as well as your daily activity. You are going to record this information in a food and exercise diary. Later on you will use these data to compile your own Health Passport.

Learning goals
In this part of the mission you will:

  • learn how to organize, analyze and interpret data.
  • learn how to perform calculations with simple formula's.
  • learn how to calculate the basal metabolic rate (BMR), and explain what the BMR is.
  • learn how to calculate the estimated energy requirements (EER), using an energy diary and a given list of activities.
  • learn how to evaluate the balance between calorie intake and use of calories by the human body.
  • learn how to explain and calculate the body mass index (BMI).
  • learn how to measure the heart rate (pulse) of a person.
