Body mass index

The Health Passport you have already been working on brings together the most important facts regarding your diet (and health). During previous activities you have already calculated the daily calorie intake, BMR and EER. Now you will calculate your body mass index (BMI). The Body Mass Index (BMI) is used to indicate whether someone has a healthy body weight. You probably remember the dietician measuring Morgan’s bodyweight in ‘Super Size Me’. To calculate the BMI, you need to know the weight (in kilograms) and the height (in metres) of a person.

What does a particular BMI value say about physical health? There are roughly four different categories:

Underweight                 your BMI is under 18.5
Normal weight              your BMI is in between 18.5 and 25
Overweight                   your BMI is in between 25 and 30
Obese                           your BMI is over 30

But before jumping to conclusions, you should be aware that these categories normally apply to adults. Your own body is still developing and growing, and your BMI might change considerably over the years.
