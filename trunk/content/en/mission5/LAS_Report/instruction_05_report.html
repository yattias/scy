05: Report

Research doesn't stop when you have drawn your conclusions. Scientists share their findings with their colleagues (and the general public) by writing papers and reports. Write a short report on the experiments that you have performed. This report must have the following chapters:

  1. introduction
    Explain the particular situation or the problem that was the starting point of your research. Provide some general background information when necessary.
  2. research questions and hypotheses
    Cleary state your research questions and hypotheses.
  3. experimental procedure
    Describe the simulations you have performed. You can show the computer model you have been working on.
  4. results of your experiment
    Briefly discuss the results of your experiments. You can also incorporate the graphs and figures you have created.
  5. conclusions
    Briefly discuss the main conclusions of your investigations. Also make some references to the general problem that was mentioned in the introduction.

After you have finished your report, you have completed your first mission. Well done!
