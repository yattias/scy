Guidelines: Research questions

  • Choose a question that is not too broad and not too narrow.
  • A question has to be researchable (e.g. you have to be able to collect the right data) and feasible using available resources (time, equipment, etc.).
  • A good research question contains one independent factor and one dependent factor.
  • "Open" questions are usually more useful than ‘closed’ questions.
  • A useful phrase could be: "what is the relation between [concept 1] and [concept 2]?"
