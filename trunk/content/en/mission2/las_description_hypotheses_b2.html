Now you begin your inquiry cycle. In this cycle you will formulate hypotheses as answers to research questions. To get started, read the problem presentation. After that, you will complete the following ELOs:

1. Problem formulation
2. Hypotheses
Guidelines for completing the ELOs are given in the drawers. You can also find other additional information in the drawers. And, of course, you can always use the ‘Glossary’.
When you have completed your second ELO – Hypotheses, you can open the mission map and begin with the Inferences space.

 

Learning goals

After successfully completing these assignments, you will be able to:

• identify problems from a narrative or video based on an authentic situation;
• search and critically analyse background information;
• formulate your research questions;
• propose hypotheses that relate dependent and independent variables;
• explain the importance of light in an ecosystem;
