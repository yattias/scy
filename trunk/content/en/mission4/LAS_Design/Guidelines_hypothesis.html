What is a ‘good’ hypothesis or anticipated result? Here are some guidelines:

  • A hypothesis/anticipated result is a clear and concise statement which expresses the expected answer to a question.
  • A hypothesis/anticipated result has to be testable.
  • A hypothesis/anticipated result does not have to be correct;
    if your hypothesis/anticipated result turns out to be false, that doesn’t mean your research was not successful.
  • Many hypotheses/anticipated result are phrased as ‘if … , then …’ statements.
