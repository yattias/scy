Checklist for crime scene examination

Create a checklist that can be used by people who collect biological samples at a crime scene. Your checklist must include the following components:

  • a short definition of DNA (10 lines maximum), in which you explain why DNA is both universal (to all living organisms) and specific (for all individuals)
  • a bulleted list of samples that can be used for DNA-profiling
  • a bulleted list of precautions that should be taken while collecting the samples in order to avoid contamination
  • an instruction on how to transport the samples to the laboratory

    • You should at least be familiar with the following concepts: DNA, base pairing, double helix, chromosome, allele and genotype.
