The Forensic SCY-Lab

Welcome to The Forensic SCY-Lab.

In the mission map you will find 2 parts.
On the left part, your expertise will be DNA profiling. Your job will be to analyse and compare DNA from traces found at a particular crime scene with DNA samples taken from the victim and a small number of suspects.
Optionnaly, you will be asked to make the right part of the mission map. If so, you will be an expert in chemical analyses, and your job will be to analyse and compare chemical traces of substances found in strategic locations: i.e. anonymous letter and suspect's house.

In order to help you, you are provided with a handbook [hyperlink] that you can consult throughout the mission. At some points, you will be provided with webnews. They will give you clues to understand the case you actually work on.

The end-result of your lab work will be an official report for the attorney general.
Be aware that the verdict of the judge will be based – at least partly – on your analysis. But if you find a match between two DNA profiles or 2 chemical substances, does that imply that the perpetrator has been found?

Good luck!

Learning goals
After completion of the mission, you will have learnt a lot about biology, mathematics, chemistry and...
  • You can extract, organize and critically evaluate information on a definite subject.
  • You can follow an experimental process to answer a question:
    - write correct hypotheses
    - design and carry out experiments related to expressed hypotheses
    - use ICT to express and exploit my results: tables, graphical representations…
  • You can take into account scientific, economic, ethical and moral standpoints. You can form an opinion on one or more personal and/or societal issues.
