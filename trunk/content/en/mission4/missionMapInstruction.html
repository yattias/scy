The Forensic SCY-Lab

Welcome to The Forensic SCY-Lab. The following lessons you will work as forensic researchers, scientists that try to answer all sorts of questions related to legal issues. You will be engaged in a real investigation and will have to:

  • give recommandation for the crime scene investigation,
  • choose the technic of analysis according to the samples you will be provided with,
  • elaborate your experimental procedure,
  • carry out the experiment and analyse your results.
    • The end-result of your lab work will be an official report for the attorney general.

    Your expertise is DNA profiling, and your job is to analyse and compare DNA from traces found at a particular crime scene with DNA samples taken from the victim and a small number of suspects.

    DNA profiling involves a lot of lab work. You have to make sure that your experimental procedure is correct, and that you work really carefully. In order to help you, you are provided with a handbook [hyperlink] that you can consult all during the mission.

    Be aware that the verdict of the judge will be based – at least partly – on your analysis. But if you find a match between two DNA profiles, does that imply that the perpetrator has been found? Your report to the judge must include a section on this issue.

    Good luck!

    Learning goals
    After completion of the mission, you will have learnt a lot about biology, mathematics, chemistry and...
    • You can extract, organize and critically evaluate data on a definite subject.
    • You can follow an experimental process to answer a question:
      - write correct hypotheses
      - design and carry out experiments related to expressed hypotheses
      - use ICT to express and exploit my results: tables, graphical representations…
    • You can take into account scientific, economic, ethical and moral standpoints. You can form an opinion on one or more personal and/or societal issues.
