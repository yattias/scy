Visual representation of data

The result of the last step in your experiment – gel electrophoresis – is a gel with band patterns. To make your results accessible and understandable for the people in the courtroom, you will take the following steps:

1. Take a picture of the gel with a digital camera or your mobile phone.
2. Upload the picture onto your computer. Then upload the picture into this drawing tool. See here [hyperlink to ‘how to take a picture’] if you need further instructions.
3. Annotate the picture with arrows and/or small textboxes to indicate:

  • which DNA-profile belongs to which donor (the sample found at the crime scene, the victim, suspect A, etc.)
  • which side of the gel contains the smaller DNA fragments, and which side contains the larger fragments,
  • which side of the gel was positively charged, and which side was negatively charged.
