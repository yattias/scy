Research questions about my field of expertise

Generate research questions

Now that you are more or less familiar with the most important concepts and factors in your field of expertise, it is time to construct a couple of research questions. A research question precisely describes something that you want to find out. But what is a 'good' research question? Formulating research questions can be quite difficult if you have never done it before. Here are some guidelines that may help you:

- Choose a question that is not too broad and not too narrow.
- A question has to be researchable (i.e., you have to be able to collect the right data).
- 'Open' questions (questions starting with words like "What", "How" or "When") are usually more useful than 'closed' questions that can be answered by a simple "Yes" or "No".
- A useful phrase could be: "what is the influence of [concept 1] on [concept 2]?"

Start your research by formulating three research questions. Additional questions can be added later on.
