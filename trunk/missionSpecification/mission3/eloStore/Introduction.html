Canteen Cuisine – A Healthy Pizza

Introduction

Canteen Cuisine addresses the need of healthy meals at school canteens/cafeterias around the world. Today, children in Europe and the United States consume more food products high in sugar, salt and fat content than thirty years ago. This led to the increase of many health problems including obesity, diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, heart related disease and other diet-related problems. Thus, the purpose of the Canteen Cuisine mission is to actively engage you in practices related to the canteen/cafeteria cuisine of your school. You will be involved in creating a healthy pizza for your school canteen/cafeteria while taking into consideration the nutritional value of the ingredients, the human digestive system, diet-related health issues and daily exercise.

Pizza is one of student’s favorite meals/snacks. However, it is also one of the unhealthiest foods since it incorporates high levels of calories and fat. Unfortunately, pizza is served in many school canteens/cafeterias around the world without any restrictions or considerations about its ingredients. Pizza, however, does not have to be unhealthy. Taking into consideration the nutritional value of different toppings and ingredients, school canteens/cafeterias can provide a more healthy option with less fat and calories and more vegetables.

Your assignment is to create a healthy pizza to be served at your school canteen/cafeteria. In order to do so you will follow a stepwise approach. First you will learn about the nutritional value of food items in general (carbohydrates, fat, proteins, energy, vitamins, etc.), and of various pizza ingredients in particular. Then you will familiarize yourself with the food pyramid and the classification of food products (grains, fruits, vegetables, milk, meat and beans), information on energy (calories) and the human metabolism. Further, you will learn how to read and interpret the label of ingredients of a products and how the digestive system works, including diet-related health problems. Finally, you will use your new knowledge to create a healthy pizza to be served at your school canteen/cafeteria. In order to move on to the next step in this mission, you must first complete the previous one. However, you will have the freedom to revisit the steps you have already completed when ever you wish.

Mission 3 addresses the following general and specific learning goals.

General learning goals
In this mission you will:

  • identify problems and formulate hypotheses.
  • design, plan and perform experiments to test the hypotheses.
  • organize, analyze and interpret data.
  • collaborate with peers (share tasks/information, collaborate on experiments, make decision together, etc.).
  • plan and monitor your own learning process.
  • reflect on your own knowledge and learning goals.
  • present in the presence of a large group (partners and/or classmates).
  • write individual reports and assignments.
  • perform simple calculations (e.g. additions and multiplications) or calculations with simple formulas (e.g. BMR and BMI).

Mission 3 specific learning goals
In this mission you will:

  • learn about nutrient, their function and their daily required quantity.
  • learn about the nutritional needs of a person (e.g. proteins, carbohydrates, calcium, minerals, vitamins, etc.).
  • learn about the caloric needs of a person based on the basic energy requirements (BER) and the extra energy requirements (EER).
  • familiarize yourself with the digestive system, its functions and processes.
  • familiarize yourself with health issues and how to combat/avoid them.
