4.3 Exercise diary

At the beginning of the mission you have created a personal diet and exercise diary for a chosen character. In this activity you are going to use the data from the exercise diary to estimate how much energy your character has spent during this period. First, you will calculate the amount of energy your character uses when his/her body is at rest. This measure is referred to as the basal metabolic rate (BMR). After that you will calculate the total estimated energy requirements (EER) of your character.

4.3.1: Basal metabolic rate (BMR)

Your body uses a lot of energy, even when it is at rest. Your heart and lungs are working, your body temperature has to be kept at a constant level, and there are many chemical processes going on in all your cells. Your basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the amount of energy you use when doing all these ‘basic’ things. For most people, this accounts for more than half of the total amount of energy they spend during a day. Not surprisingly, the BMR depends on weight and height, age and gender. The BMR is usually measured in kcal/day.

1. You already know the age and gender of your character. To calculate the BMR, you also need his/her weight and height. Estimate what would be a realistic weight and height of your character. Write these numbers down in your character’s health passport [link to SCYLab tool, see Appendix C].

2. Use the following formula to calculate the BMR of your character:

BMR = (10.0 x weight) + (6.25 x length) – (5.0 x age) + 5        (male)

BMR = (10.0 x weight) + (6.25 x length) – (5.0 x age) – 161    (female)

[where weight is measured in kilograms (kg), height in centimetres (cm) and age in years (y)]

3. Write down the BMR of your character in his/her health passport [link to SCYLab tool, see Appendix C].


Estimated energy requirements (EER)
The total amount of energy you spend on a day is of course higher than your BMR. The actual amount of calories your body needs is expressed in a value known as the estimated energy requirements (EER, measured in kcal/day). To estimate the EER of your character, you are going to calculate the amount of calories he/she has spent for each day in the exercise diary. A list of activities, and the amount of calories you burn while doing them is found here [link to spread sheet with activity list, see appendix B]. Please be aware that (an estimate of) the BMR is already included in these numbers.

APPENDIX B

1. Use the Data Processing Tool [link to Data Processing Tool] to compose a spreadsheet that looks like the example below.

  1. In the first two columns you fill in all the activities your character has performed in one particular day, as well as the duration of each activity (see the exercise diary). Please note that duration is given in hours; so 30 minutes equals 0.5 hours.
  2. Check if the total duration of all the activities is 24 hours.
  3. From the list of activities [again a link to spread sheet with activity list] you copy the estimated amount of calories burned in one hour (while performing that particular activity). Enter these values in the third column. If you cannot find information in the list, check one of the websites (http://primusweb.com/fitnesspartner/calculat.htm and
    http://health.discovery.com/centers/cholesterol/activity/activity.html), or choose another activity from the list that you feel is approximately as energy consuming.
  4. Now calculate the amount of calories burned for the duration of each activity (fourth column). Multiply the value in the second column by the value in the third column.
  5. Calculate the total amount of energy spend during that day, by adding up all the values in the fourth column. This is the EER of that particular day.

2. Follow step a. to b. for each day in the exercise diary. Make sure that you save the spreadsheets of each individual day.

3. Calculate the average EER of your character. Write down the average EER in his/her health passport [link to SCYLab tool, see Appendix C].
