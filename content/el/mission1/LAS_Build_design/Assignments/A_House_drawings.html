House drawings

Design your own CO2-friendly house

After each member of your group has become an expert in a particular field of knowledge, it is time to actually start designing your own CO¬2-friendly house. At the end of the mission you have to present your (BioEnergy’s) house design to the mayor of your city. Please remember, the reduction of CO2-emissions is the central issue. But at the same time, your house has to provide room and a reasonable level of comfort for a family of four members (two parents and two children).

Your design group has to produce a collection of drawings that include:
- drawings of the exterior of the house (front view, side view and top view),
- floor plans of the interior of the house,
- a 3D artist’s impression.
All drawings except for the artist’s impression must specify dimensions (e.g. size of walls, windows, etc.).

This is a test!

Your house drawings can be produced in different ways:
- using Google SketchUp (you can download SketchUp for free at http://sketchup.google.com),
- using other (technical) drawing software,
- using pencil and paper (and a ruler, to make sure that dimensions are accurate).
