Description of Activity 3: In this activity, you will familiarize yourself with the food pyramid (old/new) and its function. In addition, you will learn how to construct the food pyramid by placing different food products in the appropriate pyramid group. Finally, you will compare your/alter-ego diet with the daily nutritional value and the food pyramid requirements.

General learning goals and skills
In this activity you will:

  • organize, analyze and interpret data.
  • collaborate with peers (share tasks/information, collaborate on experiments, make decision together, etc.).
  • write individual reports and assignments.
  • perform simple calculations (e.g. additions and multiplications).

Domain-specific learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • learn about the concept of the food pyramid (old and new) and its purpose.
  • learn about the different groups of food products and frequency of consumption.
  • learn how to construct a food pyramid by placing different food products in the correct group.
  • learn how to assess your own eating habits (health passport).

 

3.1: Food pyramid information
During the last activity, you have learned about nutrients and their functions. Another way to look at the diet of your character, is to consider not the nutrients in it, but the food items themselves. In 1992 the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) published a dietary guide, in the schematic form of a pyramid, in order to promote a healthy diet while helping to reduce the possibilities of chronic degenerative diseases. However, because of the development of diet science and dietetics, the revision of this model of diet was inevitable. As a result, in recent years the USDA presented the new alimentary pyramid named “My Pyramid,” quite different from the old one.

Watch the following video and find the differences between the old and the new food pyramid and explain why these changes were necessary. Use the text editor tool and record your answers as a group.

3.3: The food diary of your character
Now it is time to calculate the daily caloric intake of your alter-ego/self. Please follow the instructions bellow.

3.3.1. Calculate daily calorie intake
Use the food and exercise diary you created in activity 1.4.1a or b and create a table like the example below (in SCY data processing tool):

  • Take the food diary of your character/self.
  • Make an inventory of all food items and the amounts consumed (for all three days combined).
  • Find the amount of calories per 100g for each item.
  • Use the data processing tool to calculate the amount of calories consumed (for each food item) and the total amount of calories.
  • Divide the resulting value by three in order to find the average daily amount of calories your alter-ego/self consumes.
  • Put this value in the health passport.

3.3.2. Evaluate alter-ego/self diet in terms of the food pyramid
Evaluate the eating and drinking habits of your alter-ego/self in terms of the food pyramid. Write a short text in the health passport, including a conclusions and recommendations section, about the food diet of your alter-ego/self in consideration of the questions bellow. During activity 6, you will finalize this text.

  • Does the diet include food products that contain all the necessary nutrients?
  • Does the diet include the appropriate amount of necessary nutrients?
  • Does the diet include food products from all groups according to the recommendations of the food pyramid?
  • Is the amount (percentage) of the food consumed (from each category) according to the recommendations of the food pyramid?
  • How can the diet be improved in order to be healthy?
