6.2 Food and health problems

Unhealthy food can be very tasty. Many people are fond of fast food products such as French fries, hamburgers and chicken nuggets. But eating fast food on a daily basis is not without consequences. Your teacher will show you parts of a documentary called ‘Super Size Me’. In this documentary, we follow Morgan Spurlock from New York, who engages in a bizarre experiment: he will eat only fast food (at McDonalds) three meals a day, for thirty days in a row. A number of physicians monitor his physical and mental health during this experiment. After watching the documentary there will be a classroom discussion. Finally, you will have to write short comments on a set of statements related to this topic.

6.2.1. Watch the documentary ‘Super Size Me’ (shown by your teacher).

1. There will be a classroom discussion on some of the issues brought forward by the documentary. In preparation for the discussion, write short comments on paper (50 words maximum) to each of the following statements.

  1. Diet-related health problems such as obesity, diabetes and cardiovascular diseases are a major threat to our society.
  1. Eating healthy is entirely your own responsibility.
  1. Soft drinks (such as coke or lemonade) fit well in a healthy diet.
  1. School canteens should not sell fast food products at all.
  1. Fast food restaurants (such as McDonalds) should provide customers with more information about the nutritional value of their products.

2. Participate in the classroom discussion led by your teacher.
