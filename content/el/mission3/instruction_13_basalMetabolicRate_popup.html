Basal metabolic rate (BMR)

Your body uses a lot of energy, even when it is at rest. Your heart and lungs are working, your body temperature has to be kept at a constant level, and there are many chemical processes going on in all your cells. Your basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the amount of energy you use when doing all these ‘basic’ things. For most people, this accounts for more than half of the total amount of energy they spend during a day. Not surprisingly, the BMR depends on weight and height, age and gender. The BMR is usually measured in kcal/day.
