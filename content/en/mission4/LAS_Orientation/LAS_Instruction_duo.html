Orientation

A number of samples are sent to your laboratory by the attorney general. These six samples are all related to the same murder case (case number CY0347), and include one evidence sample and five reference samples, namely:

  • a sample collected at the crime scene itself (blood trace)
  • a sample taken from the victim
  • a sample taken from suspect A
  • a sample taken from suspect B
  • a sample taken from suspect C
  • a sample taken from suspect D

    • The assignment of the attorney general is the following:

    “Possible cell material should be taken from the crime scene sample in order to produce a DNA-profile, after which the obtained DNA-profile should be compared with the profile of the arrested, or yet to be arrested, suspects and third-party (victim) in this investigation. In case no match is found, the crime scene profile should be saved in the DNA database and compared to other profiles in the database.”

    In this part of the mission you will select the techniques that you have to employ for your query.

    Learning goals
    After completion of this part of the mission…
    • you can describe how the following techniques work: DNA isolation, PCR, gel-electrophoresis, DNA-fingerprinting with restriction enzymes.
    • you can explain the role of DNA as a coding biopolymer for proteins.
    • you can explain the differences between coding and non-coding DNA.

    Orientation

    DNA profiling

    When you are a DNA expert, the assignment of the attorney general is the following:
    “Possible cell material should be taken from the crime scene sample in order to produce a DNA-profile, after which the obtained DNA-profile should be compared with the profile of the arrested, or yet to be arrested, suspects and third-party (victim) in this investigation. In case no match is found, the crime scene profile should be saved in the DNA database and compared to other profiles in the database.”

    In this part of the mission you will select the techniques that you have to employ for your query.
    Chemical analysis

    When you are a chemical analyst, the assignment of the attorney general is the following:
    “Lipstick found on a glove at the suspect’s house should be compared with several lipsticks belonging to the victim."

    In this part of the mission you will justify the choice of the TLC technique to answer to this assignment.
    Learning goals
    After completion of this part of the mission…
    • you can describe how the following techniques work: DNA isolation, PCR, gel-electrophoresis, DNA-fingerprinting with restriction enzymes.
    • you can explain the role of DNA as a coding biopolymer for proteins.
    • you can explain the differences between coding and non-coding DNA.
    Learning goals
    After completion of this part of the mission…
    • you can explain how the technique of TLC works.
    • you can explain why TLC is a good technique to analyse lipsticks.
