Information

You are an expert on DNA. Your job is to isolate DNA from the (biological) samples that are sent to your lab. The quality of these samples partly depends on how they are collected by your colleagues at the crime scene itself (the crime scene examiners). In this first part of the mission you will write a checklist for crime scene examiners on how to collect, store and transport samples.

Learning goals
After completion of this part of the mission…
  • you can describe the structure of DNA (using the concepts of nitrogen bases, sugar and phosphate backbone, double helix and hydrogen bonding).
  • you can link the concepts of DNA, chromosome, gene, allele and genotype.
  • you can explain the universality of the molecule DNA and the specificity of each living organism.

Information

You are an expert in DNA profiling or chemical analysis. Your job is to analyse samples that are sent to your lab. This can only be done successfully if you first understand the concepts related to the experiments you will carry on.

DNA profiling

When you are an expert in DNA profiling, you will write a checklist for crime scene examiners on how to collect, store and transport samples. The quality of the samples partly depends on how they are collected by your colleagues at the crime scene itself (the crime scene examiners).
Chemical analysis

When you are an expert in chemical analysis, you will make a concept map including the concepts related to the techniques that you know and that can be used in chemical analysis. As you will use Thin Layer Chromatography (TLC) in this mission, chromatography will be a central concept of the map.
Learning goals
After completion of this part of the mission…
  • you can describe the structure of DNA (using the concepts of nitrogen bases, sugar and phosphate backbone, double helix and hydrogen bonding).
  • you can link the concepts of DNA, chromosome, gene, allele and genotype.
  • you can explain the universality of the molecule DNA and the specificity of each living organism.
Learning goals
After completion of this part of the mission…
  • you can list different techniques of separation and identification of chemical substances.
  • you can relate the solubility of a substance in a solvent to its migration on a static phase with this solvent as the mobile phase.
  • you can identify chromatography as a separation and identification technique.
