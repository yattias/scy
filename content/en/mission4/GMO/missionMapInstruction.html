It was a beautiful shiny spring afternoon, when a boy and a girl were walking on a street and passing by a local supermarket. The girl said: "William, lets go in and buy some foodstuff! I would like to try making sushi. You know, yesterday when I was chatting with my American niece on Skype, I got a very good sushi recipe called "Inside-Out Salmon Sushi Roll" from her. So, let’s buy some salmon, sushi rice, avocado, nori seaweed sheets, sesame seeds and other ingredients. Then we can go to my place and make some." William thought the idea was great and they went shopping.

When they reached to the fish-section and started selecting suitable salmon, Kate said: "Do you know what my niece also has said? She told me that in her country, after some time, a first genetically modified animal for human food – a salmon – will be available for consumption! I am personally a little bit scared of these new "Frankenfoods" and as far as I have heard, they are probably dangerous. There is always so much buzz around them in the news!" William scratched his head, while saying: "I think people should not use this scary nickname for GMOs. May be they are even better than ordinary organisms and bring benefit for the humankind? How would you know?" Kate became curious: "It is so strange that growing GMOs is allowed in some countries, but is not in others. Is it reasonable to grow genetically modified salmon in our country?"
