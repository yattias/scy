Welcome to SCY-Lab!

During the mission, you will work on multiple Emerging Learning Objects (ELOs). The ELOs that you are to be working at the moment, are aligned on top of your screen.

Each ELO has four different drawers on the left side.

  • The first drawer contains assignments and guidelines related to the ELO.
  • The second drawer contains different information resources that help you to create the ELOs (e.g. texts of theory, web-sites for additional information, etc).
  • The third drawer is for asking feedback on your ELO.
  • The forth drawer is for tagging. Here you can create a tag for the ELO and agree or disagree with existing tags.

In the four corners of SCY-Lab, you can see specific SCY-services you will need throughout the mission.

  • On the top left corner you can select buddies to collaborate on a specific ELO. To invite a buddy for collaborating on an ELO, just drag and drop the icon of the buddy to the respective ELO.
  • On the lower left corner you can create new ELOs, search for existing ELOs and remove unnecessary ELOs from desktop.
  • On the top right corner, there are Feedback buttons for asking and providing feedback, and the e-Portfolio button that enables you to navigate to your online portfolio.
  • On the lower right corner you can see the mission map icon. When you click on it, the mission map will open. In the mission map, there are a number of icons that represent different parts of the mission (these parts are also called "spaces"). Clicking on an icon opens up the respective space. When you have finished working in one space, you can re-open the mission map and move to the next space. This is how you can navigate through the mission.

In each part of the mission, you will work on some ELOs. When entering a space for the first time, the special window - "curtain" - appears. The curtain provides some basic instructions about what you have to do in each part of the mission. If you have read the instructions, you can close the curtain . The curtain can be re-opened by clicking the button on the top center of the screen.

In each space, you will always find a glossary that explains the central concepts you will need to understand for accomplishing the mission.
