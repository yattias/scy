Now you are in the beginning of your inquiry cycle. First, read the problem presentation. According to that story, you have to compose three different ELOs:

1. Problem formulation

2. Research questions

3. Hypotheses

Each of the three ELOs contains guidelines that help you in completing the tasks. You can also find here texts of theory, glossary and list of web-pages for additional information. When you have formulated the last ELO – hypotheses, you can open the mission map and move next to the “Inferences” space.

 

Learning goals
After successfully completing the assignments, you are able to act as a researcher and:
  • identify problems from a narrative based on a everyday situation;
  • search and analyse critically background information;
  • formulate your research questions;
  • pose hypotheses that contain dependent and independent variables;
  • identify different trophic levels of an aquatic ecosystem;
  • explain the basic types of interactions between species (predation, competition, mutualism).
