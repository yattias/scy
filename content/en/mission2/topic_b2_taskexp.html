Experimental procedure

1. Set up the Halogen lamp.
2. Fill the beakers slowly with water (400 ml).
3. Place the beakers at different distances from the light source; the nearest beaker should be 50 cm from the lamp.
4. Weigh out the same amount of Canadian Waterweed for each beaker to be used in your experiment and put the waterweed in the beakers.
5. Set the length of the measurement period (minutes = number of beakers minus 1) and the sampling rate (60 sec) of the Vernier data collector.
6. Make sure (without recording data) that the concentration of dissolved oxygen in the water is constant (below 2 mg/l) at the beginning of experiment.
7. Start the experiment and let it run for 20 minutes.
8. After waiting for 20 minutes, put the dissolved oxygen probe into the beaker that is closest to the light bulb and the light sensor near to the beaker and start data collection in this condition.
9. As soon as data collection in the first beaker is completed (for the first time point of 0 minutes), take the oxygen probe out of the water, clean it with distilled water, and put it into the next closest beaker and the light sensor near to it (before the next measurement will be taken automatically by the Vernier device, which will be in 60 sec, as defined earlier).
10. Continue taking measurements in the remaining beakers until light intensity and concentration of dissolved oxygen have been measured in all beakers (repeat step 9).
11. If you have time, you can repeat steps 8 to 10 several times in order to increase the validity of your experiment.
12. Finally, clean all your equipment and store all as guided by the teacher.
