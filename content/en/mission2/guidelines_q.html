Guidelines: Research questions

1. First, identify the variables to include in your research questions.
2. Next, distinguish between dependent and independent variables. The former correspond to the observed results of experiments, while the latter are parameters you manipulate in order to observe their impact on dependent variables.
3. Use only one dependent variable in each research question you formulate.
4. If there is more than one independent variable that influences the dependent variable, you can include them all in the experimental procedure. However, you must change only one independent variable at a time and keep all other independent variables unchanged. This allows you to be sure that the effect you measure in the dependent variable can be attributed to the independent variable you have manipulated.
5. Choose a question that is not too broad or too narrow.
6. A question must be researchable (e.g. you must be able to collect the data) and feasible using available resources (time, equipment, etc.).
7. 'Open' questions are usually more useful than 'closed' questions.
