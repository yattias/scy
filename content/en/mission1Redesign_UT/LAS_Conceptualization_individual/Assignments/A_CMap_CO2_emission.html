A concept map is a graphical representation of important concepts (in boxes), and the relations between those concepts (represented by arrows or lines with a label). This may sound complicated, but if you take a look at the example in the 'resources' drawer, it will become clear what the main idea of a concept map is.

How to start?

To get you going, think of the following focus question:

"Which factors contribute to domestic CO2 emission (and what are consequences)?"

Your concept map should answer this question. Proceed as follows:

1. Start by creating a few concepts that are important for domestic CO2 emission.
2. Connect these concepts with each other. A link connecting two concepts should have a label: a word or a brief phrase that defines the relationship between these concepts.

3. Each concept or idea has to be connected with at least one other concept.

4. From that point on, your concept map will grow. You will probably think of new concepts that are related to the ones that are already in the concept map.

5. Of course you can always make changes to parts of your concept map you have done earlier.
