5.2 A map of the digestive system (1)

The digestive system is an organ system. The organs of the digestive system work together to break down the food you eat, and take up (absorb) the nutrients in it. Basically, the digestive system is one long hollow canal that runs through your body from your mouth to your anus. During this activity you will work on a schematic drawing of the digestive canal. Finally, you will have created your own ‘map’ of the digestive system.

1. Open the schematic drawing of the digestive canal [link to SCYLab tool with existing image, see Appendix D].

2. The first step is to put the names of the organs on the map. Two names are already given. Visit this website to find the rest of the names:

http://www.bioplek.org/animaties/spijsvertering/spijsvertering2.html

3. Put the names on the map.

APPENDIX D

Please note that the liver and the gall bladder are not represented in the drawing. These organs do however play an important role in digestion. The liver produces bile, which is temporarily stored in the gall bladder. The gall bladder releases bile into the digestive canal to make the fatty substances in your food more soluble. In addition, the liver breaks down toxic substances that enter your body through the digestive system.
