4.1 Introduction

In the previous activity you have studied food pyramids. The food pyramid is designed to show the optimal balance between different categories of food products. But you probably remember the staircase at the left side of the new food pyramid: a reference to your exercising behaviour. It is not only the composition of your diet that matters, the quantity is also important. There has to be a balance between the amount of calories in your food, and the amount of energy you spend. And that depends on the activities you undertake throughout the day.

General learning goals
Students will be:

  • able to handle large amounts of data in a spread sheet tool
  • perform simple calculations (e.g. additions and multiplications).

Specific learning goals
Students will be:

  • aware of the importance of regular exercise for their health
  • able to explain how energy is measured
  • able to give examples of processes of the human body that require energy
  • able to explain what factors the energy need of a person depend upon
  • able to calculate the basal metabolic rate (BMR), and explain what the BMR is
  • able to calculate the estimated energy requirements (EER), using an energy diary and a given list of activities
  • able to evaluate the balance between calorie intake and use of calories by the human body
