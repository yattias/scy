03: The benefits of eating pizza

Pizza is one of students’ favorite meals but it is considered to be among the least healthy products found in many school canteens/cafeterias. According to the results of a research conducted by the Word Health Organization, there is a large increase in cancer cases connected to diet while the diet of young children is connected even more with harmful and unhealthy food products that lead to obesity, as you watched in the previous video. For this reason, school canteens/cafeterias can play an important and decisive role since schools provide access to different kinds of food products for students. However, this doesn’t mean we cannot eat our favorite food. In many cases, unhealthy food products can be improved by replacing unhealthy ingredients with healthy ones. Does pizza protect against cancer?
International Journal of Cancer, 107, 283–284 (2003)

Have you ever thought about preventing cancer by eating pizza? This might sound a bit unusual or odd, however, Italian scientists (perhaps because pizza is their national food) conducted a large scale research in order to determine the relation between the consumption of pizza and cancer.

During the study, scientists examined the eating habits of 3,315 Italians who suffered from various forms of cancer, such as digestive system, larynx and pharynx (throat) cancer. They also examined 4,999 other patients, who did not have cancer but were hospitalized for other reasons. 

For each patient they did a detailed analysis of what patients usually ate while taking into consideration other factors, such as age, gender, family history, consumption of alcohol and cigarettes and physical exercise throughout their life.

The patients were categorized in three groups based on how much pizza they consumed. Those who did not consume any pizza at all were placed in the first group. In the second group were those who consumed pizza one to three times a month while the third and last group were those who consumed pizza once a week or more often. 

The findings of the Italian scientists were very interesting:

  • Those who consumed more pizza (group 3), compare to those who did not consume any pizza at all (group 1), had less danger developing mouth, pharynx, esophagus, larynx and large intestine cancer.
  • Among the 1,255 patients who had large intestine cancer, 503 of them never consumed any pizza and 473 consumed pizza on rare occasions. In contrast, of those who consumed pizza on regular bases, only 249 had large intestine cancer.
  • Between the 598 patients who had mouth and pharynx cancer, 310 never consumed any pizza and 213 consumed pizza occasionally. Of those who consumed pizza on regular bases, only 75 had pharynx and mouth cancer.

These results are impressive, however, we can not yet support the conclusion that pizza protects against cancer. While the study was well designed and several precautions were taken into consideration in order to eliminate the number of factors which lead to errors, the heterogeneity among the variety of pizza ingredients raises some concerns. In addition, the study failed to determine the longevity patients were consuming pizza.

While the study gears towards a certain direction, the exact reasons of what causes this effect must be further examined. If pizza does protect against cancer, further research must be carried out to determine the mechanism behind this relation. A possible explanation that warrants further investigation is the tomato sauce used in all pizza recipes. Previous studies have shown that the consumption of tomatoes is negatively related to certain kinds of cancer.
