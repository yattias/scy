04: Your personal food and exercise diary

You probably think you have a reasonable idea of what you eat and drink on an average day. But do you know how many grams of food you actually consume? Or how many litres of fluids you drink? Finally, how much do you exercise? Now is the time to find out. For the duration of three days, you are going to keep track of everything you eat and drink. You are going to put this information in a food and exercise diary (see the example below).

  • Note down everything you eat or drink on three consecutive days (from midnight to midnight).
  • Not only write down the name of a particular food or drink product, but also note the quantity you have consumed (in grams or litres).
  • If you consume more than one food item in a particular hour, you will have to add a new row (or new rows) for the additional items.
  • If you are not sure about the weight of a particular food item, you can either check the package or use a (kitchen) scale or measuring cup to measure it. Sometimes you can find the information you need on the internet. Once you start doing this, you will develop a certain feeling about how much things weigh (e.g. an average sized glass is about 200 mL).

Your food contains energy, which is used by your body for movement, maintaining a constant body temperature and various other purposes. You will learn more about the energy your body spends later on in the mission. To make your diary complete, you will also note down everything you do during a day.

  • Note down everything you do in three days (from midnight to midnight).
  • Not only write down what you are doing, but also how long you have been doing it (in minutes).
  • Add new rows if you perform more than one particular activity within one hour.
  • Even when you are not really exercising, but just attending class or sleeping (or both at the same time), your body consumes energy. So, you also have to note these kinds of activities.
  • If you are performing the same activity twice during one hour (e.g. walking to the video store, and 20 minutes later walking back to your house) you can of course add these two values up.

Check the example of a food and exercise diary below to see how you note down the information. Please note that the information you put in your diary is private. You do not have to share it with others.
