1.3: The benefits of eating pizza
Pizza is one of students’ favorite meals but it is considered to be among the least healthy products found in many school canteens/cafeterias. According to the results of a research conducted by the Word Health Organization, there is a large increase in cancer cases connected to diet while the diet of young children is connected even more with harmful and unhealthy food products that lead to obesity, as you watched in the previous video. For this reason, school canteens/cafeterias can play an important and decisive role since schools provide access to different kinds of food products for students. However, this doesn’t mean we cannot eat our favorite food. In many cases, unhealthy food products can be improved by replacing unhealthy ingredients with healthy ones. Read the article bellow to learn how researchers in Italy investigated whether the consumption of pizza may help prevent cancer. Then, try to answer the following questions:
1. Does the consumption of pizza helps prevent cancer?
2. Do the Italian researchers provide valid data to support their claim?
3. What are the implications of this study for the meaning of the term “unhealthy food?

 

Does pizza protect against cancer?
International Journal of Cancer, 107, 283–284 (2003)

Have you ever thought about preventing cancer by eating pizza? This might sound a bit unusual or odd, however, Italian scientists (perhaps because pizza is their national food) conducted a large scale research in order to determine the relation between the consumption of pizza and cancer.

During the study, scientists examined the eating habits of 3,315 Italians who suffered from various forms of cancer, such as digestive system, larynx and pharynx (throat) cancer. They also examined 4,999 other patients, who did not have cancer but were hospitalized for other reasons. 

For each patient they did a detailed analysis of what patients usually ate while taking into consideration other factors, such as age, gender, family history, consumption of alcohol and cigarettes and physical exercise throughout their life.

The patients were categorized in three groups based on how much pizza they consumed. Those who did not consume any pizza at all were placed in the first group. In the second group were those who consumed pizza one to three times a month while the third and last group were those who consumed pizza once a week or more often. 

The findings of the Italian scientists were very interesting:

  • Those who consumed more pizza (group 3), compare to those who did not consume any pizza at all (group 1), had less danger developing mouth, pharynx, esophagus, larynx and large intestine cancer.
  • Among the 1,255 patients who had large intestine cancer, 503 of them never consumed any pizza and 473 consumed pizza on rare occasions. In contrast, of those who consumed pizza on regular bases, only 249 had large intestine cancer.
  • Between the 598 patients who had mouth and pharynx cancer, 310 never consumed any pizza and 213 consumed pizza occasionally. Of those who consumed pizza on regular bases, only 75 had pharynx and mouth cancer.

These results are impressive, however, we can not yet support the conclusion that pizza protects against cancer. While the study was well designed and several precautions were taken into consideration in order to eliminate the number of factors which lead to errors, the heterogeneity among the variety of pizza ingredients raises some concerns. In addition, the study failed to determine the longevity patients were consuming pizza.

While the study gears towards a certain direction, the exact reasons of what causes this effect must be further examined. If pizza does protect against cancer, further research must be carried out to determine the mechanism behind this relation. A possible explanation that warrants further investigation is the tomato sauce used in all pizza recipes. Previous studies have shown that the consumption of tomatoes is negatively related to certain kinds of cancer.

 

1.4.1: Your personal food and exercise diary (optional)
You probably think you have a reasonable idea of what you eat and drink on an average day. But do you know how many grams of food you actually consume? Or how many litres of fluids you drink? Finally, how much do you exercise? Now is the time to find out. For the duration of one day, you are going to keep track of everything you eat and drink. You are going to put this information in a food and exercise diary (see the example below).

  1. Note down everything you eat or drink on one day (from midnight to midnight).
  2. Not only write down the name of a particular food or drink product, but also note the quantity you have consumed (in grams or litres).
  3. If you are not sure about the weight of a particular food item, you can either check the package or use a (kitchen) scale or measuring cup to measure it. Sometimes you can find the information you need on the internet. Once you start doing this, you will develop a certain feeling about how much things weigh (e.g. an average sized glass is about 200 mL).

Your food contains energy, which is used by your body for movement, maintaining a constant body temperature and various other purposes. You will learn more about the energy your body spends in activity 4. To make your diary complete, you will also note down everything you do during a day.

  1. Note down everything you do in one day (from midnight to midnight).
  2. Not only write down what you are doing, but also how long you have been doing it (in minutes).
  3. Even when you are not really exercising, but just attending class or sleeping (or both at the same time), your body consumes energy. So, you also have to note these kinds of activities.
  4. If you are performing the same activity twice during one hour (e.g. walking to the video store, and 20 minutes later walking back to your house) you can of course add these two values up.

Check the example of a food and exercise diary to see how you write down the information. Please note that the information you put in your diary is private. You do not have to share it with others.


1.4.2: Create a food and exercise diary
The goal of this mission is to create a healthy pizza. But in order to do that, you need to know some things. For instance, you have to be able to calculate the amount of nutrients and energy in a particular food item. You must also know how to estimate the amount of energy your body uses while performing a particular activity. You will practice all these skills during the mission. But if we want to do calculations, we need numbers. And you will create these data yourself.

  • Pick one character from the list of characters [link to list with descriptions]. This will be your alter-ego for the rest of the mission.
  • Based on the description of your character, you will create a detailed food and exercise diary. So, try to imagine what your character would eat and drink for breakfast, lunch and dinner. What kind of snacks would he or she consume (and how many)? And what kind of activities will he or she perform throughout the day?
  • Try to be as realistic as possible. You will need these data throughout the mission for further calculations.
  • Near the end of the mission (during activity 6) you will compile a health passport [link to health passport] for the character you have chosen.

  1. First check the example of a food and exercise diary to get an impression how it should look.

  3. Not only write down the name of a particular food product, but also note the quantity he or she has consumed (in grams or litres).
  4. If you are not sure about what would be a realistic weight of a particular food item, you can:

  • use a (kitchen) scale or measuring cup to measure it
  • find the information you need on the internet
