5.4 A map of the digestive system (2)

Your digestive system breaks down the food you eat, and takes up the nutrients in it. Each organ of the digestive system has its own particular function. In your mouth, your food is chewed and mixed with saliva. After you swallow, your food is grinded and pushed through the digestive system by movements of muscles in the wall of the digestive canal. Now you will turn back to the schematic drawing of the digestive system and complete the drawing with arrows that depict the secretion of digestive juices and the uptake of nutrients.

1. Open the schematic drawing of the digestive canal you have been working on [link to SCYLab tool with existing image, see Appendix D].

Food contains nutrients such as carbohydrates, proteins and fats. Most of these nutrients are large molecules that cannot be absorbed by the cells of our body. These large molecules have to be broken down into smaller molecules (e.g., starch is broken down to glucose). The digestive system breaks down large molecules into smaller ones using digestive juices that contain special substances called enzymes. These enzymes have the ability to break down particular molecules into smaller molecules.
