5.1 Introduction

During the previous activities you have learned about nutrients and their function in the human body. As you remember, the overall goal of this mission is to create a healthy pizza. But if you are eating your pizza, how do the nutrients enter your bloodstream? In this activity you will learn about the different organs of the digestive system and how they work.

General learning goals
Students will be:

  • able to explain simple information to the rest of the class.

Specific learning goals
Students will be:

  • able to reproduce the basic anatomy of the digestive system
  • able to explain the function of each organ of the digestive system, especially in terms of (a) the secretion of digestive juices and (b) the absorption of nutrients (including water)
  • able to explain that digestive juices contain certain substances (enzymes) that break down nutrients into substances that are small enough to be absorbed by the digestive system

 

5.2 A map of the digestive system (1)

The digestive system is an organ system. The organs of the digestive system work together to break down the food you eat, and take up (absorb) the nutrients in it. Basically, the digestive system is one long hollow canal that runs through your body from your mouth to your anus. During this activity you will work on a schematic drawing of the digestive canal. Finally, you will have created your own ‘map’ of the digestive system.

1. Open the schematic drawing of the digestive canal [link to SCYLab tool with existing image, see appendix D].

2. The first step is to put the names of the organs on the map. Two names are already given. Visit this website to find the rest of the names:

http://www.bioplek.org/animaties/spijsvertering/spijsvertering2.html

3. Put the names on the map.

APPENDIX D

Please note that the liver and the gall bladder are not represented in the drawing. These organs do however play an important role in digestion. The liver produces bile, which is temporarily stored in the gall bladder. The gall bladder releases bile into the digestive canal to make the fatty substances in your food more soluble. In addition, the liver breaks down toxic substances that enter your body through the digestive system.

 

5.3 A simulation of the digestive system

You are already familiar with the names of the individual organs of the digestive system. But what is the function of each particular organ? This is what you will find out during the following activity.
1. The class will be divided in seven groups. Each group will collect information about one particular organ of the digestive system. Your teacher will tell you which group you are in.

5.4 A map of the digestive system (2)

Your digestive system breaks down the food you eat, and takes up the nutrients in it. Each organ of the digestive system has its own particular function. In your mouth, your food is chewed and mixed with saliva. After you swallow, your food is grinded and pushed through the digestive system by movements of muscles in the wall of the digestive canal. Now you will turn back to the schematic drawing of the digestive system and complete the drawing with arrows that depict the secretion of digestive juices and the uptake of nutrients.

1. Open the schematic drawing of the digestive canal you have been working on [link to SCYLab tool with existing image, see Appendix D].

Food contains nutrients such as carbohydrates, proteins and fats. Most of these nutrients are large molecules that cannot be absorbed by the cells of our body. These large molecules have to be broken down into smaller molecules (e.g., starch is broken down to glucose). The digestive system breaks down large molecules into smaller ones using digestive juices that contain special substances called enzymes. These enzymes have the ability to break down particular molecules into smaller molecules.
2. Use the green arrows to indicate the places in our digestive system where digestive juices are secreted (as has been done with the mouth). If necessary, visit this website to fresh up your memory:

http://www.bioplek.org/animaties/spijsvertering/spijsvertering2.html

Once the nutrients in your digestive system are small enough, they can be absorbed by the cells of the digestive canal and enter the bloodstream. You have already learned what organs are responsible for the absorption of nutrients during the classroom simulation.
3. You have to use the red arrows to indicate the places in our digestive system where nutrients are absorbed. Use the blue arrows to indicate the places in our digestive system where water is absorbed. If necessary, visit this website to fresh up your memory:

http://www.bioplek.org/animaties/spijsvertering/spijsvertering2.html
