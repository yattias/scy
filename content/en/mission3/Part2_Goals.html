Activity 2: Food and Nutrients

Activities:
2.1: Introduction to nutrients (text/websites).
2.1.1: Create a table with most important nutrient categories: carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins, minerals; on this table the students fill in the definition, the function of each nutrient in the body (energy, building block, support, metabolism, etc.), the daily required quantity of each nutrient and provide examples of each.
2.2: Labels of ingredients.
2.3: Nutrients and energy calculations.
2.4: Optional: excursion to supermarket/grocery store.
________________________________________________

Description of Activity 2:
In Activity 2 you will be introduced to the five categories of nutrients (carbohydrates, fats, proteins, vitamins and minerals), their function and their daily required quantity. In addition, you will learn how to read and interpret nutritional value labels found on most food products.

General learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • organize, analyze and interpret data.
  • collaborate with peers (share tasks/information, collaborate on experiments, make decision together, etc.).
  • write individual reports and assignments.
  • perform simple calculations (e.g. additions and multiplications)

Domain-specific learning goals
In this activity you will:

  • learn about the definition of each nutrient, its function and its daily required quantity.
  • learn about the nutritional needs of a person (e.g. proteins, carbohydrates, calcium, minerals, vitamins, etc.).
  • learn how to read and understand the label of ingredients on food items.
  • learn how to calculate the nutritional value of a certain quantity of a product.
