Becoming an expert

You have been assigned a specific expert role:
expert on energy sources, expert on heat pumps, expert on solar panels and solar thermal collectors, or expert on heat loss and insulation.
So now it is time for you to become a 'true' expert in your field. But how do you become an expert? How do you ‘build’ knowledge?

Following these steps, your expert group builds up a lot of knowledge on certain aspects of CO2-friendly building.
Later on, each of you will be using this knowledge for the benefit of your own design group.
But for now you have to operate as a team. It might be wise to divide tasks between the members of your expert group.
But by the end of the expert phase, all findings must have been shared, so that each member has acquired more or less the same knowledge and skills.

How to start?

It is often a good idea to start with a little bit of background reading on your subject.
After you have become acquainted with the most important terms and concepts in your field of knowledge, it is time to formulate a couple of research questions.
Then you browse for information that is specific for your research questions. You have to critically examine and compare the information you gather so that you know
it holds scientific standard. When you have information of good standard that answer your research question you can draw conclusions.

Note that this circle is often considered as a 'spiral'. When you have new information it is not uncommon to go back and refine your initial research question
and then try to answer the new research questions.
